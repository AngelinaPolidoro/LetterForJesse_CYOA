Letter for Jesse

Ending: A letter in the mail.

It’s done, all your hard work has culminated on this desk in front of you. Emotion and turmoil seep from it, barely contained in the parameters of a paper. There’s almost a twinge of sadness to see something this personal go, but then there’s another emotion. The butterflies in your stomach are rampant and a dread washes over you as you grab an envelope. Fear grabs hold of you, you don’t know if Jesse will like this or even want this. They left you then and maybe they’ll leave you again.

But something sparks inside of you: hope.

You will never know if they like it if you don’t try. Even if they crumple it up and throw it away, you will get some form of closure. Years and years of seething and sorrow is much worse than the embarrassment of sending a letter. Maybe Jesse and you drifted away, and maybe they were too embarrassed to reach out to you. You take a deep breath as you put the stamp on and shakily write the address. Maybe, just maybe, Jesse will light up when they see your letter. You smile as take one more look at the final remarks on your letter:

In the past I needed you. Now I cherish you. And in the future, I will always remember you.
