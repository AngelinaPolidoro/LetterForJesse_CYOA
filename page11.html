Letter for Jesse

A somber sonnet.

You choose your words carefully, with analogies of birds migrating and promises of harvest. The bittersweet undertone of the poem is like the loom of winter. You know your poem is effective because you feel its effects hit you. The flight of dried, dulled leaves in fall still echoes through your head. But you might want something more literal, the play on emotions is nice but it could be more effective if you communicate in normal language. You could scrap the tragic poem and try again another day.
